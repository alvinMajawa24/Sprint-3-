From this website, you can easily navigate using Kenya's public transport and save money but not using uber.

Kenya's public transport system is a dynamic network that serves as a lifeline for millions of commuters and travelers across the country. At the heart of Nairobi, the capital city, lies a trio of bustling bus stations: Koja, Tearoom, and the Bus Station.

Koja, situated in the bustling central business district, stands as a vibrant hub where matatus—minibusses adorned with colorful artwork—converge, offering quick and affordable rides to various destinations within Nairobi and beyond.

Tearoom, adjacent to Koja, pulsates with energy as commuters navigate its labyrinthine alleys to catch matatus heading to different corners of the city. This bustling station serves as a crucial interchange point, facilitating transfers between different routes and providing access to diverse neighborhoods and suburbs.

Meanwhile, the Bus Station, located nearby, serves as a primary terminal for long-distance buses connecting Nairobi to major cities and towns across Kenya. Here, travelers embark on journeys that span the length and breadth of the country, from the bustling streets of Mombasa to the picturesque landscapes of Kisumu.

These iconic bus stations not only represent vital nodes in Kenya's transportation network but also embody the spirit of resilience and adaptability ingrained within the country's public transit system. Seamlessly linking urban centers and rural communities, they facilitate economic growth, foster cultural exchange, and enable individuals to access opportunities and connect with loved ones across vast distances.
